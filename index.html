Who We Are

Building Pathways for Every Student

Bending Back Barriers is a registered 501(c)(3) nonprofit committed to strengthening educational outcomes for children and teens of color in predominantly Black, low-income urban and rural communities in and around Philadelphia.

Rather than constructing new schools, we build, maintain, and expand partnerships with existing schools that serve at-risk populations — delivering direct classroom support, essential learning materials, nutritional assistance, and access to behavioral and emotional health services.

Our Full Story

Mission

We partner with schools to ensure every student has access to high-quality learning environments, essential resources, and comprehensive wellness supports.

Vision

A future in which all students, regardless of income or zip code, learn in fully resourced, supportive, and empowering school communities.

What We Do

Our Programs

Six interconnected pillars of support designed to remove every barrier between a student and their potential.

📖

Academic Support

Tutoring, project-based learning materials, and enrichment programming that reinforce instructional quality and improve student performance.

💻

Technology Access

Supplying classroom computers and tablets and expanding internet access for students without reliable connectivity at home.

🧠

Counseling & Wellness

Certified counselors and partner clinicians offering individual and group counseling, trauma-responsive services, and family support.

🍎

Nutritional Support

Collaborating with schools to provide meals and nutritional support during and beyond the school day to address food insecurity.

👗

Essential Supplies

Clothing, hygiene supplies, and emergency essentials for students experiencing hardship so they can focus on learning.

🚀

Workforce Readiness

STEM-based learning, mentorship, and career pathways that expand students' access to viable post-secondary opportunities.

The Impact

Students in under-resourced schools face compounding disadvantages — inadequate materials, food insecurity, and limited access to mental health support — that directly suppress academic achievement and long-term potential.

The Need

In Philadelphia's lowest-income neighborhoods, students are up to 4x more likely to face barriers like chronic absenteeism, food insecurity, and lack of technology — yet funding gaps leave many schools without solutions.
